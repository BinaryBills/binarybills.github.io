About Us

In a controversial study by the U.S. Public Interest Research Group Education Fund, scientific research has shown the price of textbooks increased at three times the rate of inflation.

These soaring prices for course materials that American students must acquire to assist them in pursuing their academic passions play a significant role in contributing to the United State's Student debt crisis and act as a barrier to the blossoming of low-income students.

Given the scale at which this issue affects University students, we can confidently assert that this problem impacts the University of Michigan-Dearborn students.


Our mission statement is to create a simple service that allows University of Dearborn students to sell and buy secondhand textbooks to help minimize the amount of debt accrued while pursuing higher education.

Sellers that own a college book they no longer need can get some money back from their investment, and buyers can purchase these secondhand textbooks at a price lower than their market value.

We believe in creating a circular, centralized market for secondhand University of Michigan-Dearborn textbooks to help students become closer to achieving financial freedom.
